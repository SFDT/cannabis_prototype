New regulation for cannabis businesses

With the passage of Proposition 64 (2016), San Francisco is developing a new and comprehensive commercial cannabis business regulatory structure, and we want everyone to have a chance to comment on the new structure.

As soon as the City has finalised a draft of the legislation, it will be posted here for you to comment on. It should be available by the end of September 2017.

The first comment period will last 30 days. After the comment period, the legislation will go to commissions, committees and then to the Board of Supervisors. All of your comments will be forwarded to these groups.

You can still be part of the process of developing these regulations, even after comments have closed.

Cannabis business registration

If you operate a medicinal cannabis business that cultivates, manufactures, tests, distributes or delivers in San Francisco, you can register now with the Office of Cannabis. If you register, we will check your application, zoning and inspect your business. There will probably be a fee for the inspection. The registration will only be open until October 31, 2017.

If your business meets the requirements you will be issued a temporary permit to operate a medicinal cannabis business by the City. Businesses with a temporary permit to operate will then be eligible for the State Temporary License which the State will make available on January 1, 2018.

The City intends to issue permanent permits for medical and adult use cannabis commercial activity in 2018.

The City’s process for issuing permanent permits is being decided as the legislation is debated. We will accept comments on the legislation shortly.

If you have a permit to operate a retail medical cannabis dispensary, you do not need to apply for a temporary permit at this point.

We are registering cannabis businesses by location. To complete the registration, you will need:

  • your Business Registration Certificate
  • when your business started operating, with copies of invoices, rent, utility bills and other documents to confirm the date
  • all the permits, licences and registrations your business holds for that location
  • your deed or a notarized letter from your landlord consenting to any cannabis business operations on the premises
  • zoning parcel block, which you will need to look up on the Property tab of the SF property information map
  • zoning district from the Zoning tab of the SF property information map

Register Now

If you don’t have the information we are requesting or you want to apply for operating permits for a new cannabis business, you can’t register now. Leave your email below and we will let you know when the City is registering new cannabis businesses.
